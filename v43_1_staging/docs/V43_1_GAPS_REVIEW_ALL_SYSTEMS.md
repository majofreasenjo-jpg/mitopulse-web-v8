# MitoPulse v43.1 — Gap Review Across Living, Stellar, Networked and Infrastructure Systems

## Conclusion first
After adding v43 modules, MitoPulse is substantially closer to a true living-system architecture.
However, there are still a few high-value gaps if the goal is to model all complex ecosystems:
- biological systems
- financial systems
- energy systems
- logistics systems
- critical infrastructure
- social/organizational systems
- planetary / stellar / cosmic network systems

The remaining gaps are no longer basic architecture gaps.
They are higher-order coordination and scaling gaps.

## Remaining priority gaps
### 1. Structural Reconfiguration / Morphogenesis 2.0
Needed for node birth/death, merge/split, role reassignment, topology stress response.

### 2. Reserve & Resilience Economy Engine
Needed for explicit reserve ledgers:
- trust reserve
- liquidity reserve
- maintenance reserve
- energy reserve
- buffer capacity

### 3. Regeneration / Recovery Engine
Needed for repair after containment:
- time-to-recovery
- regeneration pathways
- degraded-state persistence

### 4. Nested Systems Engine
Needed for systems-inside-systems:
- subnetworks inside networks
- regional inside global
- plant inside logistics inside market inside geopolitical field

### 5. External Field Coupling Engine
Needed for weather / market / geopolitical / gravitational overlays.

### 6. Attractor Landscape / Regime Transition Engine
Needed for multiple operating states:
- stable
- degraded
- crisis
- adaptive recovery
- collapse basin

### 7. Identity Persistence Engine
Needed to preserve continuity while nodes mutate, split or merge.

### 8. Observer Effect Engine
Needed because observation and intervention can change behavior in adversarial ecosystems.

## Final assessment
v43 makes MitoPulse a true living-system architecture in a strong sense.
The remaining work is universal-complex-systems work, not basic platform work.
